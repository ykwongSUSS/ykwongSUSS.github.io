2 Definitions, propositions and theorems

  • are usually placed in a fenced qmd div block
  • css style: border: 1pt black solid; padding: 6pt
  • math notation in TeX may be used in qmd
  • no cross referencing below. Just use qmd double asterisks for bold def.

Definition 3.4.3 A subspace of \(\mathbb{W}\) of \(\mathbb{R^n}\) is a set of vectors such that for every \(c \in \mathbb{R^n}\) and for every \(\mathbf{uv} \in \mathbb{W}\), then

  1. \(\mathbf{0} \in \mathbb{W}\)
  2. \(\mathbf{u + v} \in \mathbb{W}\)
  3. c\(\mathbf{u} \in \mathbb{W}\)

When these properties hold we say that \(\mathbb{W}\) is closed under addition and scalar multiplication.

2.0.1 Cross referencing a theorem and proof in div

  • #thm-line is used to cross reference displaying Theorem 1
  • .proof is a class in the div block
  • a tex-block is used for the displayed equation (4.5)
  • tex commands: \qquad\blacksquare and \tag{4.5} are used

Theorem 1 \[\int_0^\infty x^{\alpha-1}e^{-x/\beta} dx = \Gamma(\alpha) \tag{4.5}\]

Proof. Make the substitution \(u= x/\beta\), so that \(x=\beta u\) and \(dx = \beta du\):

\[\begin{align*} \int_0^\infty x^{\alpha-1}e^{-x/\beta} dx &= \int_0^\infty (\beta u)^{\alpha-1} e^{-u} \beta du \\[12pt] & = \int_0^\infty u^{\alpha-1}e^{-u} du \\[12pt] & = \int_0^\infty x^{\alpha-1}e^{-x} dx \\[12pt] & = \Gamma(\alpha) \qquad\blacksquare \end{align*}\]

Make the substitution $u = x/\beta$, so that $x=\beta u$ and $dx = \beta du$: 
\begin{align*}
    \int_0^\infty x^{\alpha-1}e^{-x/\beta} dx 
     &=  \int_0^\infty (\beta u)^{\alpha-1} e^{-u} \beta du \\[12pt]
   & = \int_0^\infty u^{\alpha-1}e^{-u} du \\[12pt]
   & = \int_0^\infty x^{\alpha-1}e^{-x} dx \\[12pt]
   & = \Gamma(\alpha) \qquad\blacksquare \tag{4.5}
 \end{align*}
